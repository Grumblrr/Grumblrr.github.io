Octet Rule:
Basic rule of thumb which states that atoms want to have 8 electrons in their outermost shell, even at the cost of their neutral charge.

Single Bonds:
Bonds between two atoms using only two electrons. Smallest and weakest of all bonds, but also the most stable because of the incredibly low level of reactivity in the compound. e.x. HCL, hydrochloride.

Double Bonds:
Bonds between two atoms using four electrons. Larger than a single bond but also significantly less stable; It's much more reactive than a single bond. e.x. CO2, carbon dioxide.


Triple Bonds:
Bond between two atoms that share three pairs of eletrons, or six total. Very reactive and vulnerable to compounds that take away electrons. e.x. C2H2, acetylene.
